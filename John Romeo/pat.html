Ada Beach

Ada Beach, located in the town of Ada Foah on Ghana's southeastern coast,

is a picturesque destination where the Volta River meets the Atlantic Ocean.

This unique confluence creates scenic beaches lined with palm trees and estuary islands.

The area offers a variety of water-based activities, including swimming,

sailing, fishing, and boat cruises. Visitors can also explore the estuary's rich biodiversity,

which serves as a habitat for marine turtles and numerous bird species

Accommodations range from eco-friendly beach resorts to more luxurious options.

Notable establishments include Maranatha Beach Camp, known for its rustic huts and proximity to the estuary,

and Aqua Safari Resort, offering upscale amenities and various stay packages.

Wli Waterfalls

Wli Waterfalls, also known as Agumatsa Falls, is located near the village of Wli in Ghana's Volta Region,

close to the Togo border. It holds the distinction of being the highest waterfall in Ghana and West Africa,

with a drop of approximately 80 meters.

The waterfall consists of two sections: the lower falls, which are more accessible, and the upper falls,

which require a more challenging hike. Visitors can enjoy a scenic walk through the surrounding forest,

part of the Agumatsa Wildlife Sanctuary, which is home to diverse flora and fauna,

including a large colony of fruit bats.

Lake Volta

Lake Volta, located in Ghana, is one of the world's largest artificial reservoirs, covering approximately 8,502 square kilometers (3,283 square miles).

It was formed in 1965 following the construction of the Akosombo Dam on the Volta River.

The lake stretches about 400 kilometers (250 miles) in length, extending from the

Akosombo Dam in southeastern Ghana to the town of Yapei in the north.
